Art Engineering · Season 2

Last of the
Gilded

Year 2026

The dragonfly takes shape from things we had already discarded. It carries the delicacy and beauty that have always belonged to nature — and, at the same time, the weight of everything our activity leaves behind.

The contrast is deliberate. A creature so fragile, built from what we could not throw away, keeps asking the visitor the same quiet question: what becomes of the things we discard, and who, finally, bears their cost?

· · ·
Materials
Recycled plastic bottles, salvaged wires, used pens and markers
Scale
Specimen-size — larger than any dragonfly that lived
Species
After the Emperor dragonfly (Anax imperator), declining
Process
Sorted, cut, shaped over a wire armature and bonded
Edition
One of one
