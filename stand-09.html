Art Engineering · Season 2

Last of the
Gilded

Year 2026

We gild what we no longer believe we can keep. Kings wore gold as a hedge against the loss of authority; crowns are made of the same metal as saints’ halos. This dragonfly is gilded for the same reason — because in the future she may be only a memory, and memory deserves the currency of permanence.

Extinction is not an event. It is a slow forgetting, species by species, year by year, noticed only when the forgetting is complete. The gold is our apology in advance. It is also our plea: that what is still here, if we name it and frame it and admit to loving it, might be saved by the naming.
