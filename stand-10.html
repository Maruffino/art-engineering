Art Engineering · Season 2

The City
Remembers Itself

Year 2026

A ruin is a city that has remembered it is mortal. Every wall was someone’s window, every staircase someone’s climb home. When the climate of a place makes it uninhabitable — too hot, too dry, too unstable — the city does not fall in a day. It fades through layers, each thinner than the last.

This diorama shows the layering made visible. Nine slices in acrylic: the city as it was, the city as it is, the city as it might be. Between them, the choices we have already made and the few we can still unmake. Whether the last layer arrives is a matter of what we do between now and the next five years.
