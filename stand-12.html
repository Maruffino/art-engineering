A Wall of
Accords

Year 2026 Medium Archival print on crumpled paper, mounted on black panel

These papers are the accords: the Kyoto Protocol, the Montreal, the Paris Agreement, the Glasgow Pact, the Convention on Biological Diversity. Treaties signed by presidents and prime ministers in distant rooms, under fluorescent light, after years of drafting. Pressed flat against the wall, they look like sheets of snow — cold, archival, uniform.

Each has saved a species, a forest, a layer of atmosphere — or promised to. The question this wall asks quietly is not whether we signed them, but whether we are still keeping them. The paper does not answer. The answer is outside, in the weather, and in the choices we make tomorrow morning.
