Art Engineering · Season 2

After the
Drought

Year 2026 Medium Hydroponics chamber, polyester fibre, grass

The relationship between water and life was once a conversation. Weather spoke; seed listened; a root drank and gave back. Now the conversation has grown small — measured in millilitres, in pumps, in the hum of a motor that pretends to be sky.

This garden is a patient. She survives not because of weather but despite it. The cloud above her is upholstery; her soil is plastic. She is proof that we can keep a green thing alive after we have broken the system that made green things possible. She is also the question of whether such a life, without the rain that belongs to it, is the life we meant to save.
