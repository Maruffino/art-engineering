Art Engineering · Season 2

Glass
Current

Year 2026 Medium Glass bottles, wire, resin Size 110 × 140 cm

She is carrying what we gave her. The shell is not a shell — it is the residue of summer afternoons, of parties no one thought would last this long. Green glass, mostly, and a few shards of blue that once held medicine.

Sea turtles swallow plastic because it looks like jellyfish. I wanted to reverse the gesture: to take what the sea has been asked to keep, and give it back a body. She will outlive any of the bottles that made her.

· · ·
Materials
148 recovered glass bottles, galvanised wire, clear resin, found water tray
Sourced from
Three weekends of shoreline collection, summer 2024
Process
Bottles washed, crushed, and bonded over a shaped wire armature
Species
After the green sea turtle (Chelonia mydas), endangered
Edition
One of one
