Art Engineering · Season 2

A Missing
Climate

Year 2026

Every generation thinks its weather is normal. A child growing up in 2026 cannot feel, in her body, what spring felt like in 1976. The climate of the past becomes a story in a book, told by grandparents, believed only partially.

This chamber preserves one such memory — eighteen degrees, seventy-eight percent humidity, twelve hours of diffuse light. Outside the glass, those conditions are rare now. Inside, plants do well. The piece is a small museum of a season we did not know we were losing until it was already half gone.

· · ·
Climate
Temperate humid, 18°C / 78% RH / 12h light
Plants
Georgian boxwood, bracken, moss, miniature fern (all pre-2000 regional herbarium)
Instruments
Analog pressure and temperature gauges (all 1960s Soviet manufacture)
Source
Archival climate data for this latitude, 1960–1985 averages
